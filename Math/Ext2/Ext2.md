$$\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}

$$
\text{ii)}\int{\frac{x+7}{1-x^2}dx}
$$
$$\begin{aligned}
\frac{x+7}{1-x^2} &= \frac{A}{1+x} + \frac{B}{1-x} \\
A(1-x)+B(1+x)&=x+7 \\
A(1-1)+B(1+1)&=1+7 \\
B &= 4 \\
2A &= 6 \\
A &=3 \\
\int{\frac{x+7}{1-x^2}dx}&=\int{\frac{3}{1+x}+\frac{4}{1-x}dx} \\
&=3\ln(|1+x|)-4\ln(|1-x|)+C
\end{aligned}
$$
